Once upon a time, in a lush green forest, lived a tiny squirrel named Squeaky. Squeaky loved to collect nuts, but he was very particular. He only wanted the biggest, shiniest nuts. He would often push aside smaller, perfectly good nuts in his search for the 'best' ones.

One autumn, a harsh winter arrived earlier than usual. Squeaky, who had spent most of his time searching for perfect nuts instead of gathering enough, found his hoard was quite small. Other squirrels, who had collected all sorts of nuts, big and small, had plenty to eat.

Squeaky soon grew hungry. He watched sadly as his friends munched on their varied stashes. He learned a valuable lesson that winter: it's better to have many good things than to wait for a few perfect ones.

From then on, Squeaky gathered every nut he could find, big or small, shiny or dull. He never went hungry again and understood that every little bit counts. The moral of the story is: Don't let the perfect be the enemy of the good.
